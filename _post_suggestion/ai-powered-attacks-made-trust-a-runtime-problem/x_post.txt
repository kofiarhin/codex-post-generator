AI-powered attacks made trust a runtime problem.

Deepfakes do not need to fool everyone. They only need to fool one tired person during one urgent approval.

Automated hacking does not need movie-level genius. It just needs speed, context, and enough attempts to find the sloppy edge case your backlog already knew about.

That is why Zero Trust is getting less theoretical.

The useful defense is not "trust no one" as a slogan. It is continuous verification: identity, device posture, behavior, transaction context, anomaly detection, and fast containment when normal starts acting expensive.

Fake trust scales now.
So real trust has to be checked in motion.
