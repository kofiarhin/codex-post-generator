AI-generated bug reports are becoming maintainer work.

That’s one of the least exciting side effects of AI coding tools, and one of the most real.

Some open source maintainers are now triaging issues that look polished, sound plausible, and still contain basically no trustworthy debugging.

It’s not classic spam.
It’s spam with stack traces.

The awkward part is that “developer productivity” on one side can become maintainer burnout on the other. More output is not automatically more value.

If AI-assisted contribution is going to work, the bar has to go up: reproducible reports, clearer issue templates, better contribution norms, less confident guesswork.

Open source cannot run on infinite free triage.
