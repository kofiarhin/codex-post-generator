GPT-5.5 model discourse will spend a week on benchmarks and a year on workflow reality.

If the new release is more efficient and better at coding, great. The useful question for developers is still not "is it smarter?"

It's whether the AI coding model can hold repo context, survive multi-step tasks, and produce work a team can actually review without turning the coding assistant into a second source of operational chaos.

The model race is starting to look like a workflow race.

The winner won't be the system that writes the flashiest snippet. It'll be the one that reduces debugging misery without making permissions, billing, and review overhead worse.
