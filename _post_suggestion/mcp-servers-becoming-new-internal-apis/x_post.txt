Model Context Protocol is interesting for one very unsexy reason:

it might turn AI tooling into something engineers can actually maintain.

The past year of agent demos has been a parade of fragile glue code, prompt gymnastics, mystery failures, and “tool integration” that worked beautifully right up until a real team had to own it.

That’s why MCP servers matter.

Not because they make agents feel smarter.
Because they make capabilities more explicit.
Because they move us a little closer to interfaces, contracts, and systems that can survive contact with production.

The useful future for AI agents is not more magic.
It’s fewer weird adapters and fewer meetings where everyone is debugging the ghost in the middleware.

If Model Context Protocol sticks, it won’t be because it looked impressive in a demo.
It’ll be because it made the stack more boring in the right places.
