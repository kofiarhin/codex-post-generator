GitHub Copilot usage limits are the most honest thing to happen to AI coding tools in a while.

We all pretended these products were just better autocomplete.
Then agentic workflows showed up, started running longer, branching wider, and burning tokens like a forgotten staging environment, and suddenly the real product appeared: quotas.

Pausing sign-ups and tightening limits is not a weird detour. It is the business model catching up to the demo.

Once an assistant can plan, retry, and parallelize, this stops being a UX story. It becomes compute scheduling, model availability, and cost control.

AI coding tools are not just selling help anymore.
They are selling how much autonomous work they can afford to let you do before reliability falls apart.
