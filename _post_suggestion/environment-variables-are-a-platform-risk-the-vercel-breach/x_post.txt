The Vercel breach is a useful correction to how teams think about secrets.

We still talk about secrets management like the job ends with `.env`, Vault, and “who can read prod.”

But an OAuth supply chain attack does not care where the secret came from. It cares which third-party app can stand next to the platform that stores it.

Platform environment variables are not passive config. They are part of your production trust boundary.

If an integration can touch deploy metadata, env vars, or automation hooks, that is not convenience. That is access.

A lot of developer tooling now deserves the same review discipline as IAM.
